About Piano Butler

What's on the site

Piano Butler covers piano repertoire across the major exam syllabuses used by teachers and students, including:

  • AMEB — Comprehensive Piano and Piano for Leisure, Preliminary through Grade 8, plus Certificate of Performance, AMusA, and LMusA diploma repertoire.
  • ABRSM — Initial through Grade 8, plus LRSM and FRSM diploma repertoire.
  • Trinity College London — Initial through Grade 8, plus ATCL, LTCL, and FTCL diploma repertoire.

Every piece is tagged with composer, era, nationality, key, and pedagogical focus areas, so you can filter by what actually matters when choosing repertoire — not just by grade.

Why it exists

Piano Butler was built by a piano teacher who got tired of manually cross-referencing syllabus PDFs every time a student needed a new piece. The goal is simple: make it fast to browse, filter, and discover exam repertoire — whether you're a teacher building a lesson plan, a student preparing for an exam, or an adult returning to piano after years away.

The site also includes a few extra tools built around that same goal: a repertoire recommender, a skills self-assessment ("diagnose"), and an exam preparation timeline planner.

Accuracy and independence

All repertoire data is cross-checked against the official, publicly available syllabus documents published by each exam board. Piano Butler is an independent reference tool and is not affiliated with or endorsed by AMEB, ABRSM, or Trinity College London. If you spot an error or omission, please let us know — see the contact link below.
