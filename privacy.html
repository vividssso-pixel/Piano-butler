Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 July 2026

Piano Butler ("we", "us", "the site") operates thepianobutler.com, a free public repertoire search and reference tool for piano exam syllabuses (AMEB, ABRSM, Trinity College London, and associated diploma programmes). This page explains what information is collected when you use the site and how it is used.

1. Information we collect

Piano Butler does not require an account or login to use the core search, browse, recommendation, diagnosis, or list-building features. We collect only the following:

  • Locally stored preferences: saved pieces and custom lists you create are stored in your browser's local storage. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to us unless you explicitly share a list via a generated link.
  • Contact form submissions: if you use the "Contact us", "Find a teacher", or "Teach with us" forms, the name, email, and message you provide are sent to us via a third-party form service (Web3Forms) and forwarded to our inbox. This information is used solely to respond to your enquiry.
  • Standard server logs: as with most websites, our hosting provider (GitHub Pages) and any analytics or advertising partners may automatically log standard technical information such as IP address, browser type, device type, referring page, and pages visited.

2. Cookies and similar technologies

Piano Butler itself does not set tracking cookies for core functionality. However, third-party services we use — including Google AdSense (for advertising, once approved) and any embedded YouTube video player — may set cookies or use similar technologies in your browser to serve relevant ads, measure ad performance, or enable video playback. These third parties may collect information about your visits to this and other websites to provide advertisements about goods and services of interest to you.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to this site and/or other sites on the Internet. You may opt out of personalised advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.

3. How we use information

  • To operate, maintain, and improve the search and recommendation features of the site.
  • To respond to enquiries submitted through our contact, teacher-matching, or teacher-application forms.
  • To display relevant advertising (once our advertising partnership is active) that helps keep the site free to use.
  • To understand aggregate usage patterns (e.g. via privacy-respecting analytics) so we can improve the product.

4. Third-party services

Piano Butler uses or may use the following third-party services, each governed by its own privacy policy:

  • Google AdSense — for displaying advertisements. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • YouTube (Google) — for in-page video previews of repertoire. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Web3Forms — for processing contact and application form submissions. See Web3Forms Privacy Policy.
  • GitHub Pages — for website hosting. See GitHub's Privacy Statement.

5. Children's privacy

Piano Butler is a reference tool intended for use by piano students, teachers, and parents. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children. Any contact form submission is expected to be made by a parent, teacher, or adult learner.

6. Your choices

  • You can clear your browser's local storage at any time to remove any saved pieces or lists.
  • You can opt out of personalised advertising via your browser settings or Google Ads Settings.
  • You can request that any information submitted via our contact forms be deleted by emailing us through the contact form on the homepage.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for legal/regulatory reasons. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect the most recent revision.

8. Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please reach out via the Contact us form on the homepage.

Piano Butler is an independent repertoire reference tool and is not affiliated with or endorsed by AMEB, ABRSM, or Trinity College London.
